Methodology

What we measure

PhDRank ranks doctoral programs by where their graduates land — faculty positions, postdocs, industry roles — not by reputation scores or peer surveys.

Every placement is traced to a verified, public career record. If a destination cannot be confirmed, it is excluded from scoring.

How we score

Each program receives a composite score from four components, weighted to reflect what the academic job market actually rewards.

D 40% destination quality T 35% career tier I 15% international reach R 10% cited-by rate V 5% variance bonus

Scores are normalized within each field over a rolling 10-year window.

Data sources

Three complementary sources are cross-referenced to achieve full coverage within each field.

ORCID — employment histories (CC-BY 4.0) CSRankings — Computer Science faculty econphdplacements.com — Economics placements

NULL destinations are imputed at the 25th percentile — no program is penalized for incomplete records.

Transparency

0% unknown

Every graduate's destination is scored. No program benefits from missing data.

Programs with fewer than 5 placed graduates are excluded. Scores reflect only the most recent 10-year cohort window to account for field evolution.

Full methodology, raw data, and replication scripts are published on GitHub. The dataset is archived on Zenodo under CC-BY 4.0.
